Sure, when creating a CSV file for bulk email sending, the two essential columns are "Name" and "Email". The "Name" column should contain the recipient's full name, while the "Email" column should have their corresponding email address. This format allows you to personalize your emails and ensure they are delivered to the correct recipients. Here's an example: Name,Email John Doe,[email protected] Jane Doe,[email protected] Remember, the order of the columns doesn't matter as long as you consistently use the same format throughout the CSV file.
